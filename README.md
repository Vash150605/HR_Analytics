# HR Attrition Analysis | Employee Churn Prediction & Insights

A comprehensive exploratory data analysis (EDA) and visualization project to understand employee attrition patterns in a company.

## 📊 Project Overview

This project analyzes employee data from a fictional company (IBM HR dataset) to identify key factors driving **employee attrition** (when employees leave the company). The goal is to help HR teams make data-driven decisions to improve retention.

### Key Business Questions Answered:
- What is the overall attrition rate?
- Which departments and job roles have the highest turnover?
- How does **Overtime**, **Income**, **Age**, and **Work-Life Balance** affect attrition?
- Which factors are the strongest predictors of attrition?

---

## 📈 Key Insights

### 1. Overall Attrition
- **Total Employees**: 1,470
- **Attrition Rate**: ~16.1% (237 employees left)

### 2. Department-wise Attrition
- **Sales**: Highest attrition (~20%)
- **Research & Development**: Lowest attrition (~13.8%)
- **Human Resources**: Moderate (~19%)

### 3. Critical Risk Factors
- **Overtime**: Employees working overtime have **~3x higher** attrition rate (30% vs 10%)
- **Monthly Income**: Lower income brackets show significantly higher attrition
- **Job Role**: Laboratory Technicians, Sales Representatives, and HR roles are high-risk

### 4. Other Observations
- Younger employees (<35) leave more frequently
- Poor Work-Life Balance correlates with higher attrition
- Stock options and promotions reduce attrition
